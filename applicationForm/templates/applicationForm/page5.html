Statement of alleged violation(s) of the Convention and/or Protocols and relevant arguments

You should be able to show that the matters about which you complain have interfered unjustifiably with a fundamental right. The European Court of Human Rights is not a court of appeal from national courts and cannot annul or alter their decisions, therefore you cannot just complain that a court’s decision was unfair or wrong.

An applicant must put down the gist of what he or she is complaining about. The Court cannot infer from the facts or domestic court documents what the applicant considers to be in breach of his or her rights. An applicant should try and identify those Articles of the European Convention on Human Rights which covers what he or she complains about – the Articles are framed in very plain and general terms that are easily understandable, such as ill-treatment, right to liberty, fair trial rights, family life rights, freedom of expression, etc. It is crucial to write down both the Article of the Convention and in a few brief sentences how it has been infringed – the Court cannot guess what an applicant objects to or make up the complaints for him or her.

Examples
Article Explanation
Article 6 The criminal proceedings against me lasted 9 years and 8 months for two levels of jurisdiction. Also, I was not allowed to cross-examine the witness who gave the sole evidence against me.
Article 5 I was detained wrongfully at the border police station for three days without being told why or being allowed to phone a lawyer or my family.
Article 3 The police kept me handcuffed in a cell without heating and gave me no food for three days. I became ill and needed treatment from the doctor for bronchitis.
